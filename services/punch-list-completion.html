The Property Manager's Punch List Problem

Every property manager knows the challenge: a unit needs work, the punch list is long, and the new tenant's move-in date is approaching fast. Finding a handyman who will actually work through the entire list — not just the easy items — and document completion for your records is harder than it should be.

Our punch list completion service solves this problem. We take your list, confirm scope and pricing upfront, work through every item systematically, and send you documented confirmation when finished. No chasing for updates, no forgotten items, no surprises. For property managers in Vancouver, WA and across Clark County, this reliability is the foundation of our service.

How Our Punch List Process Works

Start by emailing us your punch list in whatever format you use — a property management software export, a spreadsheet, a list in an email, or even annotated photos from a walk-through. Include the property address and any timeline constraints. We review the list, ask clarifying questions about ambiguous items, and confirm scope and pricing in writing.

On site, we work through items in a logical sequence, starting with safety and habitability concerns, then functional repairs, and finishing with cosmetic updates. This prioritization means the unit meets essential standards first. We carry standard materials for common items and coordinate material procurement for anything specialized.

When the list is complete, you receive an email summary mapping back to your original punch list. Each item is marked complete, noted with any follow-up recommendations, or flagged as requiring a specialist. Photos accompany the summary so you can verify work without a site visit. Property managers in Camas, Battle Ground, and Washougal value this documentation for their records and owner reporting.

Frequently Asked Questions About Punch List Completion

What is punch list completion for property managers?

Punch list completion is the systematic process of addressing every item on a property management work list — typically from move-out inspections, maintenance audits, or pre-lease walkthroughs. We work through each item, document completion, and report back.

What types of items appear on rental property punch lists?

Common items include drywall patching, paint touch-ups, fixture replacement, hardware tightening, caulking, door adjustments, outlet cover replacement, cleaning prep, and minor repairs. Each property generates a unique list based on condition.

How do you handle punch lists from property management software?

We accept punch lists in any format — email, spreadsheets, PDF reports, or photo-annotated lists. Send us what you have and we will organize our work to address every item systematically.

Can you complete a punch list in a single visit?

Most standard punch lists can be completed in one to two visits depending on the number and complexity of items. We aim for single-visit completion whenever possible to minimize disruption.

Do you document completed punch list items?

Yes. You receive an email summary confirming which items were completed, any items needing follow-up or specialist attention, and photos of completed work.

What happens when a punch list item is beyond handyman scope?

We flag the item, document it with photos and notes, and notify you by email. We recommend appropriate specialists while the rest of your list continues on schedule.

How quickly can you start on a punch list in Clark County?

We typically schedule within a few business days. For urgent turnovers, let us know your move-in deadline and we will prioritize your timeline.

Do you work with property managers who have multiple properties?

Yes. We regularly work with property managers handling portfolios across Clark County. We batch nearby properties for efficiency and maximum productivity.

What areas in Clark County do you serve for punch list completion?

We serve all of Clark County including Vancouver, Camas, Washougal, Battle Ground, Ridgefield, Hazel Dell, and Salmon Creek.

Can you handle both interior and exterior punch list items?

Yes. Our service covers interior items like drywall, paint, and fixtures, and exterior items like fence repairs, caulking, and hardware. We address the complete list in one coordinated effort.

How do you prioritize items on a long punch list?

We start with safety and habitability items, then functional repairs, and finish with cosmetic updates. If you have specific priorities, we follow your preferred order.

Do you provide pricing before starting a punch list?

Yes. We review your list, confirm scope, and provide pricing expectations before starting. For straightforward lists we offer flat estimates; for uncertain items we confirm as we assess.

Can punch list completion be combined with make-ready services?

Absolutely. Punch list completion is often the core of our turnover make-ready service. We combine it with lock changes, safety checks, and cleaning prep for comprehensive turnovers.

Do you bring your own materials for punch list work?

We carry standard materials for common items including spackle, caulk, paint, hardware, and basic fixtures. For specialized items we can pick up materials or use what you provide.

How do you handle punch lists for tenant-occupied units?

We coordinate scheduling through the property manager for appropriate access times. We work efficiently to minimize tenant disruption and complete all items in a single visit when possible.

What is your process for receiving and confirming a punch list?

Email us your list with the property address. We review, clarify ambiguous items, confirm scope and pricing, and schedule the work. Written confirmation before we start.

Can you add items to a punch list during the visit?

Yes. Additional items can be added with your approval. We document additions separately and confirm pricing before proceeding so there are no billing surprises.

Do you handle punch lists for newly purchased rental properties?

Yes. New acquisitions often have extensive deferred maintenance lists. We work through them systematically, prioritizing safety and habitability before cosmetic improvements.

How do you ensure nothing gets missed on a punch list?

We work through the list item by item, checking off each task. Our completion summary maps back to your original list for verification. Follow-up items are clearly noted.

How do I get started with punch list completion?
