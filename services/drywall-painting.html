Why Drywall and Paint Matter for Rental Properties

Walls are the largest visible surface in any rental unit, and their condition sets the first impression for prospective tenants. Patched holes, fresh paint, and clean surfaces signal that the property is well-maintained and cared for. Conversely, damaged drywall and scuffed, faded paint suggest neglect and drive quality tenants to look elsewhere.

For property managers in Vancouver, WA and throughout Clark County, drywall repair and painting is the most common and impactful turnover task. It transforms a worn unit into one that photographs well for listings and shows well during tours. Our service is built specifically for the rental context — fast execution, durable finishes, and neutral colors that appeal to the broadest tenant pool.

Our Drywall Repair Process

Every drywall repair follows the same careful sequence to ensure invisible results. We start by cleaning and preparing the damaged area, then apply the appropriate repair method based on the size and type of damage. Small holes from nails and picture hangers get filled with lightweight spackle. Medium holes from furniture damage or doorknob impacts get patched with mesh tape and joint compound. Large holes from tenant damage or removed fixtures get backed with a patch and built up with multiple compound coats.

After the compound cures, we sand smooth and texture-match to the surrounding wall. Clark County rental properties commonly have orange peel, knockdown, or smooth finishes, and we match each texture type carefully. Finally, we prime the repair and paint to blend with the existing wall color. The goal is a repair you cannot see.

For properties in Camas, Battle Ground, and Hazel Dell, we bring all necessary materials and equipment so work can begin immediately without delays for supply runs.

Painting Services for Rental Turnovers

Our painting service ranges from targeted touch-ups to full unit repaints depending on what your property needs. Touch-ups work well when the existing paint is relatively fresh and the color can be matched. When walls have faded significantly or multiple colors need to be covered, a full wall or room repaint delivers a cleaner, more professional result.

We recommend neutral, durable paint choices for rental properties — warm whites, light grays, and greige tones that appeal to most tenants and are easy to touch up between leases. We use quality paint products that provide good coverage and hold up to the wear rental units experience. Proper prep including priming over patches and stains ensures the paint job lasts.

We also paint trim, baseboards, doors, and door frames when they need refreshing. These details make a noticeable difference in the overall presentation of a unit, especially in high-traffic areas. Combined with our tenant damage repair and make-ready service, a fresh paint job completes the transformation of your rental unit.

Frequently Asked Questions About Drywall Repair & Painting

What drywall repairs do you handle for rental properties?

We handle all common drywall repairs including small nail hole filling, medium patch repairs from furniture damage, large hole patches from doorknob impacts or tenant damage, corner bead repair, and water-damaged drywall replacement. Every repair is finished to blend with surrounding surfaces.

Can you match the existing wall texture on my rental property?

Yes. We match common wall textures including orange peel, knockdown, smooth, and light splatter textures. Matching texture is essential for invisible repairs, and we take care to blend our work with the existing finish.

How much does drywall repair cost for a rental unit in Clark County?

Cost depends on the number and size of repairs needed. Small patch jobs are very affordable, while extensive repairs or full-room work costs more. We provide clear pricing before starting so there are no surprises.

Do you do full room repaints for rental turnovers?

Yes. When touch-ups are not sufficient due to fading, color changes, or extensive wall damage, we provide full room and full unit repaints. We recommend neutral, durable paint colors that appeal to the broadest range of tenants.

What paint colors do you recommend for rental properties?

We recommend neutral tones like warm whites, light grays, and greige shades that appeal to most tenants and photograph well for listings. Neutral colors also make touch-ups between tenants easier.

Can you paint just the walls that need it instead of the whole unit?

Yes. We can do targeted touch-ups, single wall repaints, or single room repaints when the rest of the unit looks acceptable. We will advise you on whether touch-ups will blend well or whether a larger repaint would look better.

How long does drywall repair and painting take in a rental?

Small patch and paint jobs can often be completed in a single day. Larger drywall repairs require drying time between compound coats, so they may take two to three days. Full unit repaints typically take two to four days.

Do you repair water-damaged drywall in rental properties?

Yes, once the water source has been fixed. We remove damaged drywall, replace it with new material, tape, mud, texture, and paint. If the moisture source has not been addressed, we will flag that before proceeding.

Can you repair drywall damage from picture hangers and TV mounts?

Absolutely. Nail holes from picture hangers and anchor holes from TV mounts are among the most common turnover repairs. We fill, sand, and paint these spots so they disappear completely.

Do you handle ceiling drywall repairs?

Yes. We repair ceiling damage including water stains, cracks, holes from removed fixtures, and textured ceiling patches. Ceiling work requires the same attention to texture matching as wall repairs.

What areas in Clark County do you serve for drywall and painting?

We provide drywall repair and painting throughout Clark County including Vancouver, Camas, Washougal, Battle Ground, Ridgefield, Hazel Dell, and Salmon Creek.

Can you repair textured ceilings like popcorn or stipple?

We can patch and match many textured ceiling types. For properties where removing popcorn texture is desired, we can discuss the scope involved. Texture matching on ceilings requires care but we achieve clean results.

Do you use primer before painting rental walls?

Yes. We prime drywall repairs and stained areas before painting to ensure proper adhesion and coverage. Skipping primer leads to visible patches and poor paint durability. Proper prep saves money long term.

How do you handle paint touch-ups on faded walls?

When existing paint has faded significantly, touch-ups may not blend well even with the correct color. In these cases, we recommend repainting the full wall from corner to corner for a uniform appearance.

Can you fix corner bead damage on rental drywall?

Yes. Damaged corner beads from impacts are common in rental properties. We repair or replace the damaged section, mud and sand it smooth, and paint to match. This restores clean, sharp corners throughout the unit.

Do you provide drywall and painting for new construction or additions?

Our focus is on repairs and repaints for existing rental properties rather than new construction. We excel at the repair and refresh work that property managers need during turnovers and ongoing maintenance.

How do I prepare a rental unit for your painting visit?

Ideally the unit should be empty of tenant belongings. If furniture remains, we can work around it but it adds time. We handle all masking, drop cloths, and surface prep as part of our service.

Can you paint trim, doors, and baseboards in rental units?

Yes. We paint interior trim, baseboards, door frames, and doors. Freshly painted trim makes a significant impact on the overall appearance of a unit and is often worth including in your turnover repaint scope.

Do you repair drywall cracks caused by settling?

We repair cosmetic drywall cracks from normal settling using appropriate techniques that allow for continued movement. For extensive cracking that may indicate structural issues, we will recommend a structural assessment.

Is drywall repair and painting included in your make-ready service?

Yes. Drywall patching and paint touch-ups are core components of our turnover make-ready service. For extensive drywall damage or full repaints, we can include them in the make-ready scope or schedule as a dedicated visit.
