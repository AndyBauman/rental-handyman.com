What Is a Rental Turnover Make-Ready?

A make-ready is the comprehensive process of preparing a rental unit for the next tenant after a move-out. This is the most time-sensitive phase of property management because every day a unit sits vacant is lost revenue. In Clark County's competitive rental market, a well-executed make-ready can mean the difference between a unit that leases in days and one that sits empty for weeks.

Our make-ready service covers the full spectrum of tasks that appear on a typical move-out punch list: drywall patching, paint touch-ups, fixture replacement, hardware tightening, caulking, cleaning prep, and minor repairs throughout the unit. We approach each turnover with a systematic checklist so nothing gets missed and work flows efficiently from room to room.

For property managers in Vancouver, WA and throughout Battle Ground and Camas, fast turnovers directly impact your bottom line. We understand that urgency and build our scheduling around it.

Our Make-Ready Process

We start every turnover with a clear scope of work based on your punch list, move-out inspection notes, or walk-through photos. You email us the details and we confirm what can be completed, the timeline, and any materials needed. This upfront clarity eliminates scope creep and keeps your budget predictable.

On site, we work through items in a logical sequence — starting with structural and safety repairs, then functional fixes, and finishing with cosmetic updates. This approach means the unit is habitable and code-compliant before we focus on appearance. Common tasks include patching nail holes and screw anchors, touching up or repainting walls, replacing damaged outlet covers, installing new light fixtures, tightening or replacing cabinet hardware, recaulking kitchens and bathrooms, and adjusting doors that stick or do not latch properly.

When work is complete, you receive a summary email with completion notes and photos. No chasing us for updates — proactive documentation is part of the service.

Why Speed Matters for Clark County Landlords

Vacancy is the biggest expense most landlords face. A unit renting at $1,800 per month costs $60 per day when vacant. If a slow turnover adds even a week to your vacancy, that is $420 lost. Multiply that across several units and the impact on annual cash flow is significant.
